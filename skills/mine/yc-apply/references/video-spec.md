The rules and the proof. Read before producing `06_video/notes.md` (or, on explicit opt-in, `06_video/script.md`). Sourced from `the-application-video-y-combinator.md`, Dalton's two talks, and full accepted-video transcripts.

## Hard constraints

- **≤ 60 seconds.** One minute. Not 90s, not "about a minute."
- **File ≤ 100 MB.**
- **All founders on camera.** Absent founders read as "the team isn't real."
- **No demo content.** The demo goes in the separate demo field (≤ 3 min / 100 MB). Founder video is faces + voices.
- **No marketing video, no AI-narrated slideshow, no music bed.**
- **Remote splice is allowed.** Founders in different cities can record separately and splice — confirmed in `how-to-apply-and-succeed-at-y-combinator-by-dalton-caldwell-y-combinator.md`. They do NOT need to be in the same room.

## The anti-script doctrine

Dalton and the official spec both say: **bullet points, not a script.** A read-aloud script produces robotic, Shark-Tank-voice delivery — an explicit interview/video anti-pattern. Partners are reading for genuine energy and clarity.

Garry Tan's "show, don't tell": pick a specific user, a specific problem, a specific solution; mention real users, especially paying ones; inverted-pyramid applies (lead with the answer).

The skill defaults to bullet talking notes. It writes a verbatim script ONLY if the founder explicitly opts in ("yes, draft a script for me"), and then prepends a warning that reading it verbatim is a YC anti-pattern. Indirect requests do not count as opt-in.

## Structure + timing budget (≤ 60s total)

```
Hook            ≤  5s   "Hi, we're <names>, building <one-line>."
Founder 1       ≤ 15s   name + 2-3 credential anchors (built artifacts / recognizable employers)
Founder 2       ≤ 15s   same shape
Founder 3       ≤ 15s   same shape (drop if fewer founders; expand others)
Ask / traction  ≤ 10s   one concrete traction stat + why YC now
```

Each bullet ≈ 3 seconds spoken. The skill shows the running budget next to each bullet and warns if the estimate exceeds 60s. Recommend recording 2 takes to see which flows naturally.

## Full accepted transcripts (reference rubric)

**Anja Health W22 (solo founder, ~50s):**

> "Hi, my name is Catherine Cross and I'm the founder of Anja. I was inspired to create Anja because of my brother Andrew — he had cerebral palsy from a young age and my parents looked into umbilical cord blood as a treatment. The stem cells inside cord blood are super potent and regenerative, so I want to democratize access and educate people about stem cells and other preventative-care measures. I want Anja to be the full D2C brand for all things longevity and preventative care — immune cell banking, genetic testing — but I'm starting with umbilical cord blood banking. With this product alone we've already made a couple of sales, have a growing waitlist of 2,000 parents, and a TikTok platform of ~20,000 followers. We also just made our first hire yesterday. I'd love to be part of YC to accelerate this growth and learn how to build a billion-dollar business."

**Embark / Varden Labs W16 (3 founders, full transcript):**

> "Hi I'm Alex, this is Brandon and Mike, and together with our trusty prototype Marvin we are Varden Labs. / I've been programming since I was 13 years old, I was ranked one of the top 20 programmers in Canada in high school, and most recently I worked as a software engineering intern at Khan Academy in Mountain View. / I'm Mike, I do mechanical stuff at Varden Labs — I've done mechanical stuff all my life, built my own fully custom 3D printer and electric scooter, and most recently worked at a YC alum, Thalmic Labs, as their mechanical intern. / Hi I'm Brandon, I'm doing software and electrical. Before Varden Labs I'd worked at three startups, the most recent an AI company where I was programming robots to interact with their environment. / The three of us were in the same mechatronics engineering class at the University of Waterloo, we've lived together for the last two years and worked on lots of projects together — this is by far our favorite and we're really excited to see how it's going to transform the future of transportation."

Structure: three intros, each ending on a credential anchor (Khan Academy; Thalmic Labs, a YC alum; three prior startups), plus an explicit answer to the implicit "how long have you known each other" question.

Common shape across both: natural speech, factual anchors, one clear traction beat, no jargon, no slides.

## What to refuse

- A polished verbatim script absent explicit opt-in.
- Any video plan over 60s.
- Cramming a product demo into the founder video.
- Personality coaching ("smile more," "project confidence") as a substitute for substance — surface it once as a light reminder, never as the core of the notes.
