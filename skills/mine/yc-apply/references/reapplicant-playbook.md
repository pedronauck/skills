## The core fact

Reapplying is a **positive** signal, not a stigma. ~45% of accepted S16 founders had been rejected before (mean 2 rejections); ~50% of the S22 batch had applied more than once. Partners explicitly track applicant history ("we keep track of this stuff, I promise we do" — Dalton). What they reward is **demonstrated progress** and **engagement with the prior feedback**.

## The delta-doc structure (`04_reapplicant-delta.md`)

A two-column table. Left: each concrete point from the prior rejection/interview feedback (paste verbatim). Right: what the team did about it, in ≤3 sentences with a number.

```
| Prior feedback (verbatim)                       | What we did (≤3 sentences, with a number)                          |
|-------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|
| "Not convinced the market is big enough."       | Signed 2 B2B pilots; bottom-up TAM now $X from N customers × $Y.     |
| "Unclear who builds the product."               | Technical co-founder Ray joined full-time; shipped v1 in 6 weeks.   |
| "No evidence of demand."                         | 60 paying users, 5K MRR, 35k organic signups since last apply.      |
```

If the founder was rejected without an interview (no detailed feedback), build the delta from the founder's own honest read of why ("we had no launched product") plus the progress since.

## Engaging with prior feedback

Two valid moves, both heavily weighted in the founder's favor:

1. **Disprove it** with evidence ("you flagged weak demand; here's the growth we did since").
2. **Concede and fix** ("you were right; here's what we changed").

The losing move is to ignore the feedback or argue with it. The skill audits the new application body to confirm it explicitly references and addresses each prior point.

## The "what changed" form field

The S26 form asks: *"If you are applying with the same idea as a previous batch, did anything change? If you applied with a different idea, why did you pivot and what did you learn from the last idea?"*

- **Same idea:** answer maps directly to the delta-doc — lead with the single sharpest proof of progress.
- **Different idea (pivot):** state why you pivoted and the concrete lesson from the last idea. Partners respect a founder who launched, learned, and moved — "launch before applying is a strong signal even if you pivot" (Dalton, `all-about-pivoting`).

Static EV's accepted answer is the model: *"Last time we didn't have a fully developed product but now we do. We've gained two B2B customers, people are using the app, we got incubated by NASSCOM, got office space, and won a competition."*

## Late vs early decision context

- **On-time always beats late.** The high-weight questions (impressive prior achievement, idea insight) take ~1-2 hours; submit by the deadline even if rough.
- **Late penalties** are qualitative now (responsiveness, fewer partners read, most slots filled) — do not quote the legacy "3× lower odds" figure as current.
- **Early Decision** is primarily for students or anyone with a defensible deferral reason; select the correct future batch in the form's batch selector and note the deferral reason.

## Post-mortem flow (Phase 9, auto-prompted on rejection)

If `00_meta.md` has `decision: rejected`, the next `/yc-apply` opens Phase 9:

1. Paste the rejection feedback verbatim into `10_post-mortem.md`.
2. Classify each point: addressable in 3 months / structurally hard / unaddressable.
3. For each addressable point, write the concrete plan + target metric.
4. Seed `04_reapplicant-delta.md` for the next batch so the next workspace's Phase 0 inherits it.

If `decision: accepted`, Phase 9 is skipped — congratulate and stop.
