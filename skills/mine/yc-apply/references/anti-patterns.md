## Buzzword / marketing-speak seed list

The buzzword scanner flags these. Each hit must produce a bad → good rewrite or a journaled rationale. Drawn from Graham, Garry Tan, Kevin Hale, Dalton.

```
transform the relationship    revolutioniz(e|ing)    disrupt(ive|ing)
leverage (as verb)            synergy/synergies      ecosystem
hyper-                        next-generation        cutting-edge
seamless(ly)                  empower(ing)            unlock (as metaphor)
AI-powered                    AI-native (w/o a noun)  best-in-class
world-class                   game-changing          paradigm
stakeholder(s)                holistic               frictionless
end-to-end (as buzz)          lifeblood              democratiz(e|ing) (vague)
platform (without specifics)  passionate/dedicated/driven (as founder claim)
```

Note: some of these are legitimate in context (e.g. "AI" describing a real model, "platform" with concrete specifics). The scanner flags; it does not auto-reject. The founder either rewrites or journals why the term is load-bearing.

## The 10-item "never say" list (Catherine Cross, cross-confirmed by Dalton + Hale)

Source: `what-not-to-say-to-y-combinator-how-to-get-into-yc.md`. Each comes with an accepted-example rewrite from `accepted-examples.md`.

1. **"We have no competitors."** → *"Generate Life Sciences leads the industry but neglects their low NPS, branding, social media, and the 97% of parents who've never heard of cord blood banking."* (Anja). No competitors usually means no market.
2. **"We need funding to start."** → *"We've already made a couple of sales, have a 2,000-parent waitlist, and 20,000 TikTok followers."* (Anja). Show what you did with $0.
3. **"We're still looking for a technical co-founder."** → omit, or pair with a shipped MVP that proves you can build without one.
4. **"We're in stealth."** → omit unless there is a genuine deep-tech IP reason. Stealth reads as fear.
5. **"AI-powered platform for revolutionizing X."** → *"Hera makes professional motion graphics accessible. The only alternative today, Adobe After Effects, is complex and time-consuming."* (Hera).
6. **"leveraging / synergies / ecosystem / stakeholders."** → plain verbs and concrete nouns.
7. **Any TAM ≥ $1B without a bottom-up calc.** → "N customers × $X/yr × Y% = $Z."
8. **"Some users / a few customers / several."** → exact numbers: *"60 paying users, 35k organic waitlist signups, 5K MRR."* (Hera).
9. **Equity split with any founder < 30%.** → 50/50 or close; lopsided splits are a red flag to partners.
10. **Fluffy story instead of numbers.** → lead with one number, not a narrative arc.

## Written-answer anti-patterns

- Leading with a problem essay before stating what the product is. (State the product first.)
- Market size top-down only, no bottom-up sanity check.
- Growth rate without an absolute base ("we grew 200%!" — from what?).
- Team section that names advisors or investors. (Nobody cares about your advisors. Founders only.)
- "Well-designed and easy to use" framed as the moat. (Not an insight — every competitor claims it.)
- Conflating monthly and annual revenue. (Dalton: "automatic disqualification" if intentional.)
- Naming a marquee company as a customer without backing it. (Same disqualifier.)
- Hiding obvious flaws. (If the partner thinks of a problem you didn't mention, they assume you didn't think of it.)

## Founder-bio anti-patterns

- Adjective-only claims: "dedicated," "passionate," "exceptional," "gives 100%."
- The startup itself listed as the most impressive achievement.
- Role-as-achievement: "led product at X" instead of "built/shipped Y."
- Credentials in place of artifacts. (List "cool things you've made," not where you went to school.)

## Founder-video anti-patterns

- Longer than 60 seconds.
- Not all founders on camera.
- A read-aloud script (robotic / Shark-Tank voice).
- A product demo crammed into the founder video. (Use the separate demo field.)
- A marketing video / AI-narrated slideshow.
- A 20-minute "spirit journey" instead of 1 minute. (Dalton's explicit example of not following directions.)

## Interview behavioral anti-patterns

- Memorized speeches / Shark-Tank delivery. (The single most common interview-killer.)
- Adversarial posture toward the interviewer.
- Robotic recited answers.
- Relying on a demo to explain the product instead of explaining in words.
- Overconfidence / denying any real challenge ("everything is perfect"). Read as lack of self-awareness.
- Bullshitting instead of saying "I don't know" or "we haven't figured that out yet."
- One founder dominating; preventing a co-founder from speaking; contradicting each other live.
- Catherine Cross's "speak arrogantly / come across as a narcissist" advice — DO NOT propagate. It contradicts Jessica Livingston's co-founder-dynamics screen and Dalton's earnestness doctrine. Use her *examples*, not her *posture*.

## Process anti-patterns

- Submitting a late application when on-time was achievable. (The high-weight questions take ~1-2 hours.)
- Doing another accelerator as a strategy to get into YC. (Sam Altman: materially harder afterward.)
- Cold-emailing YC partners or hiring "advisors who can get you in." (No warm-intro path exists; the only exception is a YC-portfolio-founder recommendation.)
- Submitting two applications for two ideas in one batch.
- Pre-incorporating in Delaware / getting a visa before applying. (YC handles incorporation at acceptance.)
